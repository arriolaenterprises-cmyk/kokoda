Where You'll Be

9-Day KOKODA TRACK ITINERARY

Journey through history, nature, and endurance.

Day 01

Arrival & Briefing

Airport transfer and hotel check-in. Welcome dinner, tactical briefing, and meeting your guides and porters.

Day 02

Owers Corner to Uaile Creek

Walk through the Gold Archway. First descent into the jungle and introduction to the trail rhythm. Distance: ~6km

Day 03

Uaile Creek to Isurava

Climb to Imita Ridge and visit the Isurava Memorial. A day of deep historical significance. Distance: ~8km

Day 04

Alola to Misima

Steep ascents and descents through valleys. Experience the silence and beauty of the rainforest. Distance: ~10km

Day 05

Via Eora Creek

Tough walking along razorback ridges. Cross rushing rivers and see stunning waterfalls. Distance: ~12km

Day 06

Myola & The Gap

Reach Myola Lake. A flatter walking day to recover your legs and enjoy the views. Distance: ~9km

Day 07

Templeton's Crossing

Walk through dense jungle and learn about the military strategy of the campaign. Distance: ~11km

Day 08

Brigade Hill

Visit the Brigade Hill Memorial. Terrain becomes slightly easier as you approach the finish. Distance: ~10km

Day 09

Arrival at Kokoda Village

The final stretch! Flag raising ceremony and celebration. Transfer back to Port Moresby. Distance: ~8km

WHAT TO PACK

Essential gear checklist for a comfortable journey.

👟

Footwear & Clothing

  • Waterproof hiking boots (broken in)
  • 6-8 pairs of thick wool socks
  • Quick-dry t-shirts & shorts
  • Long trekking pants
  • Fleece jumper & thermals (cold nights)
  • Heavy-duty waterproof jacket
🎒

Equipment

  • 30-40L Backpack + rain cover
  • Trekking poles (highly recommended)
  • Head torch + extra batteries
  • 2L Water bottle or hydration bladder
  • Microfiber towel
🧴

Health & Toiletries

  • Insect repellent (DEET based)
  • High SPF Sunscreen & Lip balm
  • Personal medications & painkillers
  • Blister pads & First Aid kit
  • Toilet paper & wet wipes
  • Hand sanitizer
📄

Documents

  • Passport / ID
  • Travel Insurance documents
  • Cash (Kina or USD)
  • Camera / Phone & charger

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Your Questions Answered

The track is physically challenging. It involves 96km of steep terrain, hot humidity, and muddy conditions. A good level of fitness and training is required to enjoy the experience fully.

The dry season (May to October) is ideal. The trails are firmer, weather is cooler, and there are fewer leeches. However, the track is beautiful year-round.

Porters carry up to 10kg-12kg of your personal gear. You will carry your own day pack (water, camera, raincoat) which is usually around 3kg-5kg.

Facilities are basic and natural. Toilets are long-drops or bush facilities. Showers are cold mountain water from streams. It is part of the authentic adventure experience!

Yes, safety is our priority. Our guides are trained in first aid and wilderness safety. We have strict safety protocols and emergency evacuation plans in place.
